A little introduction into this website is how philosophy impacts my life in so many ways as a student. What is the question of life? Is it advantageous to be just? What does it mean to be good? Are the actions of humans determined by their personalities or are the personalities of humans determined by their actions? These debates have gone on for thousands of years and yet are still left completely unanswered. That's because we may never know what that right answer is. We can debate for as long as time wants us to, but we we may never truly find out the moral construction of our minds.
Some Philosophers that will be featured on this website are: Socrates, Plato, Aristotle, Pythagoras, Thales, Herodotus, Euripides, Sophocles, Aeschylus, and more. Think of Renaissance painter Raffaello Sanzio da Urbino's, (or Raphael's) painting, the School of Athens. It is actually the background for this website. It features a great number of mathemeticians, astronomers, artisans, philosophers, and scholars, many of whom are more than just one thing on that list. That's what it means to be someone of the "Renaissance". You are a Jack of All Trades of sorts, embody many talents and skills to occupy your time with. These artisans are precisely that: Renaissance.
Pythagoras is most famous for his theorem that a² + b² = c², but he was also a philosopher. The same goes for Thales, who is famous for a theorem of his own. These people, though they came before the Renaissance, were the Renaissance. Of course, by the Renaissance, I mean the Italian Renaissance in the 1400s. This is the period of artists like Michelangelo, with his beautiful works like the Sistine Chaple, Raphael, with the magnificent and aforementioned School of Athens, and Leonardo DaVinci with the highly acclaimed Mona Lisa. These people were similar to the philosophers of the past because they shared the same hunger for knowledge and taste for different things. Writers like Dante, Christopher Marlowe, and Thomas More wrote with the pen of adventure, unafraid to boldly take the first step into traversing uncharted territory with a newfound ink of rebirth.
Fun fact: Did you know that the word "philosopher" drives from the greek words "φιλος", meaning "friend" as an noun or "dear" as an adjective, and "σοφος", meaning "wise" or "clever".
